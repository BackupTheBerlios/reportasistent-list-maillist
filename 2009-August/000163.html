journeys, I have had an opportunity of comparing on the spot his
geological sections from the valley of the Piauhy with the Amazonian
deposits. There can be no doubt of the absolute identity of the
formations in these valleys. Having arranged the work of my assistants,
and sent several of them to collect and make geological examinations in
other directions, I myself, with the rest of my companions, proceeded up
the coast to Para. I was surprised to find at every step of my progress
the same geological phenomena which had met me at Rio. As the steamer
stops for a number of hours, or sometimes for a day or two, at Bahia,
Maceio, Pernambuco, Parahiba, Natal, Ceara, and Maranham, I had many
opportunities for observation. It was my friend Major Coutinho, already
an experienced Amazonian traveller, who first told me that this
formation continued through the whole valley of the Amazons, and was
also to be found on all of its affluents which he had visited, although
he had never thought of referring it to so recent a period. And here let
me interrupt the course of my remarks to say, that the facts recorded in
